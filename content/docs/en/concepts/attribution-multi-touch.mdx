> Attribution answers one question: "who deserves credit for this sale?" But depending on the model you pick, the answer changes radically.

## The problem

Imagine a real customer journey:

```
Day 1: Marie discovers your brand via a Meta ad.
Day 3: She comes back via your email newsletter.
Day 5: She searches "your product review" on Google and clicks an organic result.
Day 7: She clicks a Google Ads ad and buys.
```

**Which channel deserves credit for the sale?**

- Google Analytics 4 (and most tools by default) says: **Google Ads, 100%**.
- But if you'd cut Meta 7 days ago, Marie would never have heard of you.
- If you'd stopped your newsletter, she'd have forgotten you.
- If you hadn't ranked on SEO, she wouldn't have found the reviews.

That's **the last-click lie**.

## What is multi-touch attribution?

Multi-touch attribution distributes credit for a conversion **across multiple touchpoints** in the customer journey, instead of attributing everything to a single one.

With Marie's same journey:

| Model | Meta | Email | Google Organic | Google Ads |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Last-touch (GA4 default) | 0% | 0% | 0% | 100% |
| First-touch | 100% | 0% | 0% | 0% |
| Linear | 25% | 25% | 25% | 25% |
| Time-decay | 10% | 20% | 30% | 40% |
| Position-based | 40% | 10% | 10% | 40% |
| Data-driven (ML) | 35% | 25% | 10% | 30% |

**Same journey, 6 different realities.** The model you pick determines where you put your marketing budget next month.

## Why it changes your decisions

If you believe Google Ads drives 100% of your sales (last-touch view):
- You increase Google Ads budget
- You cut Meta because "it doesn't convert"
- You stop the newsletter

3 months later: your Google Ads sales drop, because nobody is discovering your brand anymore. You broke your top-of-funnel.

**Multi-touch reveals the true contribution of each channel.** You stop cutting discovery channels that, on the surface, "don't convert directly".

## How Ajnix implements attribution

Ajnix captures every **touchpoint** a visitor has:

- Landing URL with UTMs
- Source, medium, campaign (auto-classification)
- Click IDs from 6 platforms: Google (`gclid`), Meta (`fbclid`), TikTok (`ttclid`), Microsoft (`msclkid`), LinkedIn (`li_fat_id`), X (`twclid`)
- Referrer, landing page, device, timestamp

A visitor can have multiple touchpoints across multiple sessions. When they convert, Ajnix looks at every touchpoint in a 30-day window and applies the chosen attribution model.

### Free version: last-touch

By default, Ajnix credits 100% to the last touchpoint before conversion (30-day window). That's the most common model, used by GA4 and most tools.

### Pro version: 5 additional models

- **First-touch**: 100% to the first discovered channel
- **Linear**: equal credit across every touchpoint
- **Time-decay**: more credit to touchpoints closer to conversion
- **Position-based**: 40% to first, 40% to last, 20% split in the middle
- **Data-driven (ML)**: Markov algorithm trained on your own journeys

## Which model should you pick?

There's no single right answer. It depends on your business.

### Last-touch is enough if

- Short purchase cycle (minutes to hours)
- Only one main acquisition channel
- Little retargeting, little email marketing
- Impulse product (a $20 fashion accessory)

### Multi-touch is essential if

- Long purchase cycle (several days or weeks)
- Multiple active channels (Meta + Google + email + SEO)
- Active retargeting
- Considered purchase (>$100, or B2B)
- You want to optimize a channel mix

<Callout type="tip">
My recommendation: compare multiple models for the same period. If top channels differ a lot between models, that's a sign your journey is complex and multi-touch brings real value.
</Callout>
