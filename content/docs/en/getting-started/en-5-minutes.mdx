> This tutorial takes you from zero to a usable first attribution report in under 5 minutes. No technical skills required.

## What you'll do

1. Install Ajnix (2 min)
2. Verify tracking (30 sec)
3. Define your first goal (1 min)
4. See your first data (30 sec)
5. Read your first report (1 min)

## Step 1: Install Ajnix

Go to **Plugins > Add New**, search for "Ajnix", click Install then Activate.

If you're lost, see the [detailed installation guide](/docs/getting-started/installation).

## Step 2: Verify tracking

Once Ajnix is activated, open your site in a private (incognito) tab and visit a few pages.

Go back to **Ajnix > Overview** in your WordPress admin. You should see:

- "Visitors today" counter: at least 1
- "Sessions today" counter: at least 1
- Traffic graph with the pages visited

<Callout type="tip">
If nothing shows up after 2 minutes, try clearing your site's cache and refreshing the Overview page.
</Callout>

## Step 3: Define your first goal

A goal is an action that counts as a conversion in Ajnix. Examples:
- Contact form submission
- Visit to a specific page (e.g., /thank-you)
- Click on a CTA button
- Scroll to 75% of a blog post

### Create a URL goal

The easiest to start with. We'll track visits to a thank-you page.

1. Go to **Ajnix > Goals**
2. Click **New goal**
3. Select the type **URL visited**
4. Fill in:
   - **Name**: "Contact submission"
   - **Pattern**: `/thank-you`
   - **Mode**: "contains"
   - **Value**: 0 (or a monetary estimate if it's a lead)
5. Click **Activate** then **Save**

<Callout type="note">
You can have up to 5 active goals on the free version. For more, you'll need to upgrade to Pro.
</Callout>

## Step 4: See your first data

Visit `/thank-you` on your site (in incognito mode if you're already tracked).

Go back to **Ajnix > Attribution** or **Ajnix > Goals**. You'll see:

- 1 conversion recorded on the "Contact submission" goal
- The marketing source that drove this conversion (direct, organic, referral, or your UTM channel)

## Step 5: Read your first report

The Attribution dashboard shows you:

- **Total conversions**: how many times your goal was hit
- **Total revenue**: sum of conversion values (for WooCommerce, that's purchase revenue)
- **Top sources**: the channels that brought the most conversions
- **Top campaigns**: the UTM campaigns that converted

### Example interpretation

If you see:
- 100 total conversions
- Google Ads: 64 conversions
- Email: 24 conversions
- Organic: 12 conversions

This model (last-touch by default) says that **the last click before conversion was Google Ads 64% of the time**.

<Callout type="warning">
This model hides a lot. A visitor may have discovered your brand via Meta 3 weeks ago, returned via email, and finally clicked a Google Ads ad to buy. Last-touch credits 100% to Google, while Meta and email played a critical role.

That's why multi-touch attribution (Pro) changes everything. [See the 5 other models](/docs/concepts/modeles-attribution).
</Callout>

## What you have now

- Ajnix installed and active
- Working tracking across your site
- 1 goal defined and capturing conversions
- First readable attribution report
